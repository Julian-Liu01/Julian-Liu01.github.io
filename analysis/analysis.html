Structural Analysis

Thesis Statement

In the first section [Sec. #1], the highlighted sentence show that both genders are facing even more serious mental health problems than before during the COVID-19 pandemic, especially anxiety and depression, which will discuss in the rest of the essay divide into two different parts: female and male. Base on the above reason, highlighted part is the thesis statement.

If you're feeling anxious and depressed right now, you're not alone. Psychological distress, depression and anxiety were prevalent among both men and women half a year into the Covid-19 pandemic and well beyond the initial lockdown periods, according to a new paper published Thursday in Lancet Regional Health-Americas.

Essay Outline

  • [Sec. #2] - Causes of anxiety and depression
    Author briefly quoted the explanation of experts to help reader quickly understand if worry is intrusive beyond the stressor will become anxiety, and if the sadness is a mood that you can't shift beyond the situation will lead to depression.
  • [Sec. #3] - Higher risk of anxiety for women
    Due to the role most woman play in modern society, like household and caretaker.They take on not only their fears, concerns and anxieties, but also those around them. Women have to burden more emotional stress than man, which make them more easily suffering from anxiety.
  • [Sec. #4] - The main factor of mental problem for men
    Also cause by gender role, man usually are the financial mainstay of the family. During the lockdown period,higher levels of financial stressors make it easier for man to getting depression compare with woman.
  • [Sec. #5] - Methods to ease psychological distress
    At the end, author give some advises and introduce some methods confirmed by psychologists that can help people overcome this period. Also indicated that the most important thing is move our minds off what is stressing us and into the moment.

Take-home Message

In the end of the essay, the author pointed out that during this period people should pay more attention about self care and mental health. The author also share some methods that can help people stay away from psychological distress, including mindful meditation or journaling. Those events let us move our minds off what is stressing us and into the moment. That is why the author quote the advice of Dr. Duffy: It's also important to engage in activities you used to enjoy before you felt distressed and set aside dedicated time for self-care.

"The focus it takes to list each of these things is often just enough focus to move our minds off what is stressing us and into the moment," said Breland-Noble, who was not involved in the study.It's also important to engage in activities you used to enjoy before you felt distressed and set aside dedicated time for self-care, Duffy said. Some examples of self-care include journaling, walking with a friend or watching a favorite television show, he said.If anyone continues to struggle with their mental health, Duffy recommended they seek professional mental health support such as counseling.
